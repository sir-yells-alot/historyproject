Modern internet usage
In 1990, ARPANET is decommissioned. Tim Berners-Lee and his colleagues at CERN develop hypertext markup language (HTML) and the uniform resource locator (URL). Allowing the world wide web to be created. In 1995, the golden year for the internet: Microsoft launches Windows 95; Amazon, Yahoo, and eBay all launched; Internet Explorer launches, and Java is created, allowing mobile “write once, run anywhere” programs to be created, and run on the internet. In 1996, Congress passes the Communications Decency Act to combat the large amount of “Non-standard” material on the internet. John Perry Barlow responds with A Declaration of the Independence of Cyberspace. Google was founded in 1998. In 1999, the music and video piracy controversy intensifies with the launch of Napster. The first internet virus capable of copying and sending itself to a user’s address book was discovered in 1999.
