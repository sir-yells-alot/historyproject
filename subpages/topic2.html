The Early Internet
In 1991, the internet changed again. That year, a computer programmer called Tim Berners-Lee introduced the World Wide Web: an internet that was not simply a way to transfer data between point A to point B, but was a “web” of information that anyone on the internet could see. Berners-Lee created the internet as we know it. The same year, congress decided that the web should be used for commercial purposes as well. As a result, companies rushed to set up websites of their own, and entrepreneurs began to use the internet to sell goods directly to customers. More recently, social media sites like Facebook have become a popular way for people to stay connected.
